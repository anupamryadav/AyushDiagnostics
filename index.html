Haematopathology Section

Haematopathology & Hemostasis are the branches of pathology which studies diseases of hematopoietic (blood) cells & blood components. The Hematopathology laboratory provides diagnostic evaluation of blood, bone marrow, and related biological samples for diagnosis of specific haematological diseases including Anemias, Leukemias/Lymphomas, Thalessemias, Haemoglobinopathies, etc - See more at: http://www.metropolisindia.com/services/pathology-testing-services#sthash.60rzloia.dpuf

Biochemistry section

Clinical chemistry is the area of clinical pathology that is generally concerned with analysis of blood (Whole blood, serum, plasma), urine, body fluids for screening, diagnosis and monitoring of many diseases.

When is it used? Tests done in clinical chemistry are varied and largely used for cardiovascular disorders, endocrine disorders like diabetes, thyroids, kidney disorders, cancers, infertility, pregnancy, nutritional disorders (Vitamins, heavy and trace metals), new born screening, autoimmunity and allergy, infections, etc.Tests are designed to detect these changes qualitatively or quantitatively and compared to the biological reference Interval of human beings.

Clinical Pathology

Clinical Pathology is a division of Pathology dealing with serology ,urine ,stool, sputum and examination of body fluids like pleural,ascitic and cerebrospinal fluid (c.s.f)

LABORATORY SERVICES AND TESTS

Complete Blood Count/CBC

The CBC is used as a broad screening test to check for disorders as anemia, infection, and many other diseases. It is actually a panel of tests that examines different parts of the blood and includes the following:

  • Hemoglobin (Hb)
  • White Blood Cell Count (WBC)
  • Red Blood Cell Count (RBC)
  • Platelet Count
  • Hematocrit (HCT)/Packed Cell Volume (PCV)
  • Mean Corpuscular Volume (MCV)
  • Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin (MCH)
  • Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin Concentration (MCHC)

Fever Profile I

  • CBC
  • ESR
  • MP
  • Widal
  • SGPT
  • Urine Routine
  • EDTA whole blood

Blood Sugar (Glucose) Test

This test is done to determine the level of sugar in the blood. It is advisable to do this test fasting (8-10 hrs) and then post meal (exactly 2 hrs after completion of meal). High blood sugar indicates that you are a diabetic.

Semen Analysis

A semen analysis is used to determine whether a man might be infertile-unable to get a woman pregnant. The semen analysis has many parts and tests a lot of aspects of the semen and sperm.

TSH (Thyroid-stimulating hormone)

TSH estimation is used to screen for and help diagnose thyroid disorders; to monitor treatment of hypothyroidism and hyperthyroidism.

Surgical Pathology

Histopathology is "the branch of medicine concerned with the changes in tissues caused by disease" - the microscopic anatomical changes in diseased tissue. It helps in making the diagnosis, provides prognostic information and guides management of patients to ensure accurate and timely care of clinical problems encountered by patients. Histopathology is a rapidly changing specialty with new immunohistochemical and molecular methods coming into routine use on a continual basis.
